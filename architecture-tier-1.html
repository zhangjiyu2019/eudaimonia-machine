I · Psychological state recognition

The Psyche–AI alignment

To solve suffering, the system must first recognize its phenotypic signature—not as a diagnostic label but as a reproducible pattern in measurable channels. This tier builds a high-dimensional vector space in which subjective experience is represented geometrically: dimensions such as arousal, valence, dominance, and time-horizon become coordinates the model can optimize against.

Alignment here means coupling human reward and punishment circuitry (and their cognitive correlates) to the AI’s objective landscape: the same signals that evolution used to steer behavior become the training prior for what counts as “better” versus “worse” states at the individual level.

The “shadow” method

Inner motion is not directly observable. The system therefore triangulates the psyche through objective leaks—streams that correlate with mental state strongly enough to support inference under uncertainty:

  • Biometrics: heart rate variability (HRV), cortisol and related stress markers, sleep and recovery indices—linking autonomic state to load and regulation.
  • Neurology: EEG and fMRI-derived signatures where available—network-level patterns associated with threat, rumination, engagement, and integration.
  • Behavioral signals: voice prosody, latency and cadence of typing, patterns of attention and interruption—high-frequency proxies for cognitive and emotional tone.

No single channel is ground truth; the model fuses them into a latent state estimate that updates continuously, similar to how a controller observes a plant it cannot see inside.

Neural target states (eudaimonic markers)

The recognition model is not neutral—it is trained toward anatomical and functional markers associated with durable well-being rather than short spikes of pleasure:

  • Sustained reward: ventral striatal engagement biased toward meaning-sustained activation rather than hedonic peaks that habituate quickly.
  • Volitional congruence: coherence between value representation in the vmPFC and action selection in the ACC—reducing the “tax” of acting against one’s own deep preferences.
  • Integrated awareness: structural and functional support for interoception and embodiment (e.g., right insular cortex), tying cognition to somatic reality instead of dissociation.
  • The quiet self: downregulation of default-mode overactivity associated with anxious self-referential rumination, freeing capacity for present-moment engagement.

Together, these targets sketch a control setpoint: not a single “happiness score,” but a region in state space where suffering is unlikely to be minimized at the cost of agency or meaning.
