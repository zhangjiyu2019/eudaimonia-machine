Web resources

External writing and tools we find useful—OSINT, spatial intelligence, AI agents, and how public data composes into actionable understanding.

Curated resources
  • The Intelligence Monopoly Is Over — Bilawal Sidhu on reconstructing major events from open data, layered on a 3D globe, and how AI agents collapse what used to take quarters into days. spatialintelligence.ai
  • Google Earth — Interactive 3D globe: satellite and aerial imagery, terrain, and layers for exploring the planet at every scale—useful ground truth for spatial reasoning and OSINT-style context. earth.google.com
  • Illustris — Large cosmological simulation of galaxy formation: high-resolution volumes, physical models, and public data—useful for thinking about emergence and structure at planetary-to-cosmic scales. illustris-project.org
  • Blue Brain Portal — EPFL’s hub for simulation neuroscience: models, software, atlases, and data toward digitally reconstructing and simulating brain tissue—grounding “neural telemetry” thinking in open research. portal.bluebrain.epfl.ch
  • The Venus Project — Non-profit exploring resource-based, systems-level habitat design: ecology, materials, energy, and resilience—adjacent thinking to post-scarcity cities and humane infrastructure. thevenusproject.com
  • AI 2027 — A concrete, quantitative scenario on how frontier AI, compute scaling, and geopolitics might unfold through 2027—forecasts, timelines, and governance stress tests from Daniel Kokotajlo, Scott Alexander, and collaborators. ai-2027.com
  • The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis — A macro “memo from the future” scenario (not a forecast): how abundant machine intelligence could stress white-collar labor, consumer demand, intermediation, and credit—useful for left-tail economic and policy thinking. Citrini Research and Alap Shah. citriniresearch.com
  • Huawei Global Industry Vision (GIV) — Long-range industry outlook on intelligent connectivity, digital infrastructure, and ICT trends—useful context for how major vendors frame compute, networks, and the intelligent world. huawei.com
